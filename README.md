# Analysis-of-the-Impact-of-SNS-Behavior-on-Social-Commerce-Purchase-Intention
# 📱 SNS Behavior & Social Commerce Purchase Intention
### *Published in the International Journal of Software Innovation (IJSI) · Vol. 10, Issue 1 · 2022*

---

> ### 💡 *"Does how you scroll determine whether you buy?"*
> This peer-reviewed study reveals that **actively engaging on social media (posting, commenting, sharing) significantly increases purchase intention**, while **passively scrolling and lurking actually suppresses it** — with social capital as the key mechanism in between.

## Why This Research Matters

Social commerce is reshaping how the world shops:

- 🛒 **83%** of online shoppers are willing to share shopping information with friends
- 📣 **67%** of online shoppers buy more based on their online community's recommendations
- 📲 Platforms like Instagram, Facebook, TikTok and Snapchat have become full commerce ecosystems

Yet a critical gap existed in the literature: **no study had examined whether the *type* of SNS behavior — active vs. passive — directly shapes purchase intention.** This study fills that gap.

---

## Research Questions
```
1. Does SNS behavior (active vs. passive) significantly influence
   purchase intention in social commerce?

2. Does social capital (bonding & bridging) mediate this relationship?
```

---

## Core Concepts

### SNS Behavior — Two Types
```
🟢 ACTIVE SNS Behavior
   └── Posting · Commenting · Liking · Sharing · Joining groups
   └── Uploading videos · Content creation · Meeting new people online

🔴 PASSIVE SNS Behavior
   └── Reading posts · Lurking · Scrolling without engaging
   └── Consuming content without communicating
```

### Social Capital — Two Forms
```
BONDING Social Capital
   └── Strong ties · Emotionally close · Family & friends
   └── Provides: Emotional support

BRIDGING Social Capital
   └── Weak ties · Diverse connections · Mutual benefit groups
   └── Provides: High-quality information
```

## Key Findings

### Active scrollers buy more — passive scrollers buy less
Increase in active SNS usage directly raises the probability of purchase. Passive-only users, on the other hand, showed *lower* purchase intention — they consume content but don't convert.

### Active behavior builds both types of social capital
Posting, liking, and sharing builds **both bonding and bridging capital** — meaning active users develop richer, more influential networks that nudge them toward purchasing.

### Passive behavior only builds bridging capital
Lurkers build weak-tie networks (bridging) but never form the emotional closeness (bonding) that drives stronger purchase influence.

### Social capital is the pathway — not just a byproduct
Both bonding and bridging capital **partially mediate** the active-SNS-to-purchase path. Bridging capital **fully mediates** the passive-SNS-to-purchase path, explaining *why* passive users show suppressed purchase intent.

## Practical Implications for Business

**For Marketers & Social Commerce Platforms:**

| Insight | Action |
|---|---|
| Active users convert at higher rates | Design features that incentivise engagement (polls, Q&A, comment threads) |
| Social capital drives purchasing | Build communities, not just follower counts |
| Passive lurkers still reach purchase intent via bridging capital | Use information-rich content to convert passive audiences |
| Platform type shapes social capital type | Tailor strategy per platform: Facebook (bonding) vs. Twitter/X (bridging) |

## Theoretical Contributions

This study advances three theoretical traditions:

**Social Capital Theory** — demonstrates that SNS behavior *generates* measurable social capital, which in turn shapes commercial outcomes.

**Social Influence Theory** — shows that compliance, identification, and internalization on SNS platforms translate directly into purchase behavior.

**Social Interaction Theory** — confirms that passive consumption lacks the interactive reciprocity needed to generate purchase intent.
